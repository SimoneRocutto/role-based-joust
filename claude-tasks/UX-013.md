# UX-013: Role-based — ability indicator design (hidden from opponents)

**Priority**: P2 | **Status**: TODO | **Tags**: `NEEDS-INPUT`

## CRITICAL CONSTRAINT: Phones face OPPONENTS

Phones are chest-mounted, screen facing outward. The player CANNOT see their own screen. Other players CAN. Therefore:

- **Ability buttons, cooldown indicators, and charge counts MUST NOT appear on the phone screen** if they would reveal the role or strategy to opponents.
- A visible "TAP TO ACTIVATE BLOODLUST" button on the phone would tell everyone "this player is the Vampire."
- Even a generic "ability ready" indicator reveals that the player has an active ability, which narrows down their possible role.

## Problem

Roles with tap-activated abilities have no feedback mechanism. The player doesn't know when their ability is available, when it's on cooldown, or that they even have one (beyond the initial role reveal).

## Open question (NEEDS-INPUT)

How should abilities be communicated given the chest-mounted constraint?

1. **Vibration patterns** — phone vibrates when ability is ready, different pattern when on cooldown. Tactile-only, invisible to opponents.
2. **Audio through earbuds** — TTS whisper "ability ready" or a distinctive sound cue. Only the player hears it.
3. **Subtle screen cue that's meaningless to opponents** — e.g., a very faint border glow that doesn't obviously indicate "ability." Risky — opponents may still decode it.
4. **No active indicator** — players just tap when they want to use their ability. If it's on cooldown, nothing happens. Simple but opaque.
5. **Combination** — vibration for "ready" + audio confirmation on activation.

Also need to clarify: which roles have tap-activated abilities vs passive?
- Check `server/src/models/roles/` — look for `onAbilityUse` or `onTap` overrides.

## Implementation plan (after input)

1. **Server**: Ensure ability state (ready/cooldown/charges) is tracked and communicated.
2. **Client**: Implement chosen feedback mechanism (vibration API, audio cue via earbuds, or subtle visual).
3. **Tests**: Test ability feedback triggers correctly for each role type.

## Dependencies

## Estimated scope

Medium — requires understanding each role's ability type and implementing the chosen feedback channel.
