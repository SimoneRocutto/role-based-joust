# UX-011: King mode — crown/king indicator on dashboard

**Priority**: P1 | **Status**: TODO

## Problem

In Long Live the King, the king is the single most critical player — their death eliminates the entire team. Yet no crown icon, highlight, or label marks any player as king on the dashboard. Spectators cannot identify who the key player is.

## Affected modes

long-live-the-king only

## Audit references

- King: `08_dash_active.png` — R-D05

## Solution

Add a crown icon or "KING" badge to the king's player card on the dashboard. This should be:
- Visible from 3-5 meters (large enough icon, high contrast)
- Distinct from other player cards (highlighted border, crown emoji, or dedicated label)
- Present during active gameplay

The phone already shows a crown emoji for kings — the dashboard should match.

## Implementation plan

1. **Client dashboard**: In the player card component, check if the player is a king (data from `game:tick` or `team:update`)
   - Add a crown icon/emoji next to the player name
   - Optionally highlight the card (gold border, slightly larger, or elevated)
2. **Server**: Verify that `isKing` is included in the dashboard-facing player state. It's already sent in `game:tick`.
3. **Tests**: Unit test that king player card renders crown. Test that non-king cards don't show it.

## Dependencies

UX-003 (dashboard HP bars) may change card layout — coordinate if doing both.

## Estimated scope

Small — add a conditional crown icon to the dashboard player card component.
