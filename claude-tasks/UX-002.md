# UX-002: Phone active HP display — ALREADY IMPLEMENTED

**Priority**: N/A | **Status**: DONE (pre-existing)

## What exists

The phone active screen already has HP visualization:
- **Battery-style background color** — the full-screen background acts as an HP indicator. Green = high HP, transitions to red = low HP.
- **"100%" text** in the top-right corner shows the numeric HP value.

## Why the audit missed this

All screenshots were captured with bots at 100% HP (bots use `botBehavior: "still"` so they never take damage). The auditor saw a solid green screen and concluded "no HP information visible." The green background IS the HP indicator — it just wasn't demonstrated at partial HP levels.

## Action: improve screenshot infrastructure

Rather than a code change, the fix is to capture **mid-damage screenshots** in future audits:
- Use `POST /api/debug/bot/:id/command` with `{ command: "damage", amount: 50 }` to bring a bot to ~50% HP before capturing
- This will show the background color shift and prove the HP system works
- Add this to the screenshot scripts or audit instructions

## UX audit instruction gap

The UX audit skill (`docs/ux-rules.md` or the auditor prompt) should note that:
- The phone background color IS the HP indicator (battery-style, green→red gradient)
- Screenshots at 100% HP will look like "just a green screen" — this is expected, not a missing feature
