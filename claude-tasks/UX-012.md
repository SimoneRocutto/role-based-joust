# UX-012: Role-based — role info visibility (NOT on phone)

**Priority**: P1 | **Status**: TODO | **Tags**: `NEEDS-INPUT`

## CRITICAL CONSTRAINT: Phones face OPPONENTS

Phones are chest-mounted, screen facing outward. The player CANNOT see their own screen. Other players CAN. Therefore:

- **Role names MUST NOT appear on the phone screen.** Showing "NINJA" on a player's chest reveals their secret role to everyone in the room. This would break the core mechanic of role-based mode.
- The phone screen is for opponents, not the player. Think of it as a jersey/badge.

## Problem

In role-based mode, the player's role is shown only during the brief role reveal sequence (audio + screen flash). After that, there's no persistent reminder. The audit flagged this as a concern, but the audit didn't account for the chest-mounted constraint.

Given the constraint, the question becomes: **where and how should role information persist?**

## Open question (NEEDS-INPUT)

Options for persistent role awareness:

1. **Dashboard only** — show roles on dashboard player cards (see UX-014). Spectators and the admin can see all roles. Players rely on the initial reveal + memory.
2. **Audio reinforcement** — periodic TTS reminders ("You are the Ninja") through earbuds only. Other players can't hear.
3. **Dashboard + audio** — both of the above.
4. **No persistence** — the initial reveal is enough. Players must remember their role. Adds tension.

The user should decide based on the desired game feel. Option 1 is the simplest. Option 2 adds immersion. Option 4 is the most hardcore.

## Implementation plan (after input)

Depends on chosen approach. If dashboard-only, this merges with UX-014.

## Dependencies

UX-014 (dashboard role display) — closely related, may merge.

## Estimated scope

Small if dashboard-only. Medium if audio reinforcement is added.
